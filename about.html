Our Story

Who we are

Ed Shemen International School was founded as a private school in Lira City, northern Uganda. It aims to provide an alternative curriculum for the region, which predominantly uses the Ugandan national education system. Until now, there was no international school in the north of the country.

The school’s vision is to create a learning space for both expatriates and nationals who want their children educated using an international curriculum. Many of these parents have been sending their children to Kampala to access schools with international curricula, particularly the Accelerated Christian Education Curriculum.

What is in a name?

Ed Shemen is derived from Hebrew, meaning "Olive Tree" or "tree of oil." It originates from a root word meaning "to shine" and signifies richness, anointing, fruitfulness, and oil. The name also connects to “Shemesh,” the Hebrew word for the sun, symbolizing brilliance.

The olive tree, one of the oldest and most valuable trees in the Bible, first appeared in Genesis 8:11, when the dove Noah sent returned with an olive branch. Since then, the olive branch has been a symbol of peace, making the name deeply meaningful.

Grade Levels

Play Group (Nursery Baby Class)

Playgroup is for babies, toddlers, and preschool-aged children. It provides a welcoming, supportive environment for all children, fostering creative and unstructured learning through play. Play is essential for physical, social, emotional, language, and cognitive development, helping young children explore and understand their world.

Pre-School (Nursery-Middle to Top Class, and Primary 1)

Pre-School is a voluntary classroom-based program for children below five years of age. It plays an essential role in early childhood education, providing children with foundational skills through structured learning and interaction.

Kinder (Primary 1)

Kindergarten is a preschool educational approach that combines play, singing, drawing, and social interaction to ease the transition from home to school. Introduced by Friedrich Fröbel in the 18th century, this approach has become a cornerstone of early-years education worldwide.

1-12 Grades (Primary 2 to Senior 6)

ESIS accepts students globally, offering flexible grade placement based on age and academic background. We provide a supportive, non-selective environment, enabling students of diverse academic levels to achieve their full potential with guidance tailored to their needs.
